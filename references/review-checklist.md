# Review Checklist

Before delivery, verify:

1. The content brief is present and sufficient.
2. Every visible scientific claim is traceable to user input or source material.
3. The central visual claim is clear within 5 seconds.
4. The slide has one dominant visual flow.
5. The composition uses a clear hierarchy between large core frame, support frames, and micro labels.
6. The main visual area is compact without crowding; the title and bottom message are not oversized.
7. Equal-size workflow cards are avoided unless the paper is explicitly a workflow paper.
8. Module text is short; paragraph bullets are moved out of the body whenever possible.
9. All text is editable PowerPoint text.
10. All visuals are native PowerPoint vectors.
11. No `ppt/media` assets, picture objects, OLE objects, external links, audio, or video are present.
12. No unsupported numbers or mechanisms were invented.
13. The palette is one of the approved presets.
14. Red/orange is reserved for defects, risk, warnings, or specific scientific meaning.
15. Chinese and bilingual text obeys language hierarchy and length rules when applicable.
16. The quality audit script has no blocking issues.


## Information density review

Check before delivery:

- Does the figure contain 7-20 semantic information units according to the selected density profile?
- Does every unit support the central claim?
- Is visible text within the Chinese or English budget?
- Are there more than two labels in any non-core module?
- Does the figure contain at least one verified output, validation cue, metric tag, mechanism object, or application cue?
- Are repeated icons, repeated arrows, and decorative panels removed?
- Can the figure still be read after manuscript-scale reduction?
