# Quality Standard

A publication-grade graphical abstract must be neither empty nor overloaded. It should have one visual core, up to four main modules, a clear mechanism or transformation, controlled labels, and a coherent academic style.

Reject designs that are:
- simple rows of identical cards;
- generic icon lists;
- text-heavy posters;
- crowded five-module pipelines;
- decorative scenes without mechanism;
- visually attractive but scientifically vague.

Accept designs that have:
- one dominant core;
- no more than four main modules;
- meaningful scientific visual objects;
- a visible mechanism or transformation;
- a result or evidence cue when provided;
- restrained colors, aligned geometry, readable typography, and balanced whitespace.
